# Outreach DM Drafts (Reddit)

> Pain-led but human and non-spammy. Reddit users despise sales pitches — lead with *their* exact problem, be transparent you're building something, offer free design-partner access, low-pressure ask.
>
> ⚠️ **Reddit etiquette:** DMs to people you've never interacted with can feel cold/spammy and risk reports. **Best practice:** first leave a genuinely helpful reply in their thread, then a soft DM. Don't paste the same message to everyone. Don't link-drop a store/app (against r/shopify rules) — talk about the problem, not the product, until they're interested.

## Reusable template
> Hey — saw your post about [their exact problem, in their words]. I've been talking to a bunch of Shopify makers hitting the same wall: [one-line restate of the pain]. I'm building a small tool focused specifically on this — [shared stock / BOM] that **doesn't oversell, even on Basic plan**. Not selling anything; I'm looking for a few real merchants to shape it (free while we build). Would you be up for a 15-min chat about how you handle it today? Either way, happy to share what's worked for others with this.

## After they reply (qualifying questions)
1. How many orders/month? (pricing tier fit)
2. Which Shopify plan? (Basic = validates the wedge)
3. What have you tried, and exactly where did it fail?
4. Has overselling actually cost you money? (quantify the pain)
5. Would you pay $19–$59/mo for something that just works?
